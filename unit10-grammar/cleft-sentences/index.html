10  Creating emphasis and cleft sentences

1 Question words

Use What / Who / How / Why / Where + clause as the subject of the sentence to pull one piece of information into focus.

How the agency will respond to these requests is unclear.

2 Nouns with premodifiers

the (+ premodifier) + thing / fact / point / problem / reason / issue + be (that) — often to frame an evaluation or summary.

The underlying problem has always been the council’s reluctance to spend money on public transport.

3 Emphasising a person or thing

It + be + noun phrase + relative clause — a classic wh- or that-cleft.

In the play, it’s May who notices that the necklace has been stolen, whereas in the film version it’s her maid.

4 Negative Not

Not a + noun for dramatic focus (often with passive or existential flavour). Typical of literary style.

Not a day goes by that I don’t think of what could have happened differently.

Your line — fix & emphasise

Nowadays he thought more about what might be in the water beneath him. — tense: nowadays goes with a present habit, not past thought.

✓ Neutral: Nowadays he thinks more about what might be in the water beneath him.

The same idea re-weighted with the four patterns above (linking colours):

1 · Wh- subject
What he thinks about more nowadays is what might be in the water beneath him.
2 · Noun frame
The plain fact is that nowadays he thinks more about what might be in the water beneath him.
3 · It-cleft
It’s he who thinks more nowadays about what might be in the water beneath him.
4 · Not a + noun
Not a day goes by nowadays without his thinking more about what might be in the water beneath him.
What the colours signal in Exercises 1–2
(a) factual detail / location
(b) emphasis on absence / “not any”
(c) what someone is thinking about
(d) stating a problem or issue

Exercise 1 — emphatic structures

  2. Not a single crime novel deals with commonplace low-level events.
