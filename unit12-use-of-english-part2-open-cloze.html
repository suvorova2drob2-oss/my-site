Modern living places great demands (0) ON our time, and as we try to balance work or school pressures with leisure and social commitments, is often our sleep that suffers. We would spend fewer hours sleeping than give up time dedicated to family, friends and free-time activities. Yet this disregard for the importance of sleep is putting our health risk, and can lead to serious issues such as heart disease, obesity and diabetes.

A recent study carried at NYU School of Medicine has identified a number of widely held myths which contribute to the problem. Most damaging of all is some people's belief that they can by on just five hours' sleep and suffer absolutely harmful effects. Also, it seems, taking naps when you routinely have difficulty sleeping at night not the solution. The researchers, led by Rebecca Robbins, PhD, suggest create a regular sleep routine for ourselves, and aim to sleep for at least seven hours a night.
