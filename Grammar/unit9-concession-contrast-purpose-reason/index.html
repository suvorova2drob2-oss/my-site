Textbook reference (examples)

Model wording. In windows 1–5 each card has full-sentence examples; your job is the same structure with your own wording.

Concession

However + adjective + subject + verb However ugly it may be, the tower is a landmark. However surprising this may seem, the interior is warm. Whatever / whoever + subject + verb Whatever you may think of the façade, the gallery is world-class. Whoever they may be, critics agree the lighting is harsh.

Contrast (neither / nor + inversion)

Neither / nor + be + subject The first scheme was not cheap, nor was the second. Neither / nor + auxiliary + subject + verb The architect did not compromise, nor did the client.

Purpose

In order (not) to + infinitive They exposed the ducts in order to signal honesty. So as (not) to + infinitive The gallery kept walls raw so as not to compete with the art.

Reason

As / since (reason) As the budget was tight, they chose plywood over marble. As / while (time + reason) While the debate raged, the team froze the finishes. Now that / as / since (present result) Now that the scaffolding is down, the proportions read clearly. In that (formal explanation) The scheme is radical in that it rejects ornament altogether. As for myself (personal stance) As for myself, I prefer working in one of those quiet courtyards.

How to run the session

  • 5–7 minutes: read the reference aloud (choral / round).
  • Windows 1–5: phase A, then B. On-screen examples are a guide; learners produce their own lines.
  • Tab 6: SPEAK — timed stems from the book; pair work or solo; flip for an optional model line.
  • Feedback: inversion after nor, no because in the purpose sprint, natural concession.
