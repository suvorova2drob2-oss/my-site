Use the verb in brackets in a suitable form

Gaps (1)–(3) are done for you. Complete (4)–(15). You may need the present perfect simple, present perfect continuous, or past simple. Where two words are given in brackets, use both (e.g. even + walk).

We (1) bought our car just after we got married, so we (2) have had it for over ten years now. Unfortunately, just recently it (3) has been giving us problems. I (4) (take) it to the garage for repairs twice this year already (last month I (5) (have) to pay for a new gear box), and for the last few weeks the engine (6) (make) a strange noise, especially when the car turns a corner. As a result, I (7) (travel) to work by bus, because I’m not sure the car will get me there safely. I (8) (even / walk) a couple of times last week. The thing is, we (9) (save) to buy a new car, but my husband (10) (just / lose) his job, so we’ll probably have to get a second-hand one instead, with the money we (11) (put) aside so far. For the past week or so, I (12) (look) online, and my husband (13) (go) to second-hand car showrooms. We (14) (not / find) anything we like yet – or that we can afford – but my brother (15) (phone) just a few minutes ago to say he might have something for us.
