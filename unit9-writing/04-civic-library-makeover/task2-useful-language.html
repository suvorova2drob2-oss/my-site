Task 2 — Useful language (from the book)

Complete the same structures that appear in The new Civic Library. The phrases signpost a topic, show contrast, or add nuance — useful in both writing and speaking. Then use Task 3 to try them in a spoken summary.

  • Parallel in the coursebook: e.g. When it comes to financial matters ≈ In terms of aesthetics.
  • Regarding the issue of… ≈ As for whether …

A. Signalling a key point / new topic

aesthetics, local designers have worked wonders on everything from the ornate bookcases to the funky chairs in the reading zone.

Three-word frame for “when we look at this aspect…”

the library serves its purpose as a place to read and work, the answer is more nuanced.

Sets up a question you are about to address.

B. Expressing contrast

The exterior has been preserved in all its former glory, the addition of a huge glass dome has upset several architectural aficionados.

As for whether the library serves its purpose as a place to read and work, the answer is more .

Not a blunt yes/no — it needs qualification.

Stunning vast bookshelves look, they leave little space for study.

Inversion: Stunning as the bookshelves look … (concession with fronting).

Libraries are just as vital as other services, in a less tangible way.

One formal word: “yes, … but in a different mode”.
