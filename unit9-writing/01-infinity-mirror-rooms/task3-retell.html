Task 3 — Retell the text (key words)

Cover the same content as the model review, but in your own words. Use the numbered prompts below for order and ideas. You do not need to use every word as a direct quote; they are anchors so you do not miss a part of the story. Aim for about 150–220 words (8–12 sentences) in a neutral / slightly formal style, like a short spoken report.

Key ideas (in story order)

  1. Opening of the show: venue, timing, the artist’s name and status. Tate Modern long-awaited opened (last week) Yayoi Kusama one of the world’s most celebrated artists
  2. How the exhibition was received in broad terms; the title and main themes of the work on display. great display big / nothing short of a success Infinity Mirror Rooms infinite space endless replication
  3. What visitors walk through, and how it connects to the artist’s long career in New York and beyond. two immersive mirror room installations conceptual minimalist arrived onto the New York art scene 1950s
  4. What the photographs add: studio, context, and how we read her as an artist. photographic works documenting sculptural work New York studio context interpret artistic force
  5. The emotional side of her move to the city: mood and a hard period in her life. emotional moving to New York alienation difficult period
  6. How the show is put together in the building. curation layout and flow throughout the gallery
  7. The “main draw” and the strongest visual effect: light, space, the whole of Tate. main draw installations suspended lights shining / radiating in all directions fill the (large) building beyond the confines of the rooms
  8. Who the exhibition is for, and a closing line on the artist’s gift. well worth a visit fans installation art lovers capture the hearts talented famous

Your retelling

Tip: First cover the list mentally in order; then write without looking, if you can. Use past tense for events (“opened”, “explored”) and present for what is still on (“The show offers…”). Draft saved in this browser.
